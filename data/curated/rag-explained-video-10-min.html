Video Summary

This video explains Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), a technique that enhances Large Language Models by connecting them to external knowledge sources.

Key Concepts Covered

  • The Problem: LLMs have a knowledge cutoff date and can hallucinate facts
  • The Solution: RAG retrieves relevant context from external sources before generating responses
  • Vector Embeddings: Converting text to numerical representations for semantic search
  • Vector Databases: Storing and searching embeddings efficiently (Pinecone, Weaviate, etc.)

Why RAG Matters

  • Provides up-to-date information beyond training data
  • Reduces hallucinations with grounded context
  • Enables domain-specific knowledge without fine-tuning
  • More cost-effective than training custom models
