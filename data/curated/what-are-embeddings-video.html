Key Concepts Covered

  • What are Embeddings: Dense vectors that represent data in a high-dimensional space
  • Semantic Meaning: Similar items are placed close together in embedding space
  • Dimensionality: Typical embeddings have 384 to 1536 dimensions
  • Embedding Models: OpenAI, Sentence Transformers, Cohere, etc.

Applications of Embeddings

  • Semantic Search: Find relevant documents by meaning, not keywords
  • Similarity Detection: Compare text, images, or other data
  • Clustering: Group similar items together
  • RAG: Retrieve relevant context for LLM generation
  • Recommendations: Find similar products, articles, etc.

How Embeddings Work

  • Text is tokenized and passed through a neural network
  • The output is a fixed-size vector regardless of input length
  • Cosine similarity measures how close two embeddings are
  • Vector databases enable fast similarity search at scale
